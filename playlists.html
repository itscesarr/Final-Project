College Playlists

Before coming to Michigan, Apple Music was my trusted companion for music streaming. But as I settled into this new chapter of life, countless friends urged me to give Spotify a chance, promising its unmatched ability to curate music that feels personal and profound. Reluctantly, I decided to repent and covert to Spotify. Although the playlist is purley reggaeton; it’s a time capsule of my first semester. It captures the Latin energy of my first frat party, the lighthearted joy of exploring a new campus, and the simple moments of fun as I began building connections in my vibrant community. It’s a soundtrack to a time when life felt refreshingly uncomplicated—a season of laughter, and finding my place at Michigan.

Late Night Drive is a playlist derived from the first time I visited the east coast during Spring Break of 2023. Even after break, I stuck with this playlist and continued to add more as I went to Mexico for the summer. The playlist is a combination of calm rap, hip-hop, and EDM. Next time you commute somewhere distant, add these songs to your queue as you get immersed in a world of soothing beats.

In Spanish slang, "Cansao" means "tired"—a word that perfectly captures the exhaustion of heartbreak. This playlist embodies the raw emotions of a breakup, weaving together the bittersweet essence of love and loss. Spanish, often hailed as one of the most passionate languages, amplifies the depth of these feelings—each lyric a confession, every melody a wound laid bare. It’s a time when you don’t want to do anything. You can’t muster the energy to go out, to leave your bed, or even to dream. Solo estas cansao.
